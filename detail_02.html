Professional Introduction of Engineering Management

Ⅰ. Goals for Professional Training
(1) Professional Profile: This major began to recruit undergraduates facing the country since 1986. In 1996, it was identified as the key construction discipline in Jiangxi Province, and was approved as professional brand of Jiangxi Province in 2002. Since 1995 the major began to recruit graduate students of the first-level discipline, management science and engineering, finally forming a multi-level pattern of learning masters, professional masters and undergraduates. (2) Professional Training Goals: This major trains high-quality, versatile personnel who adapt to the needs of socialist modernization and have integrated development in moral, intellectual, physical and other aspects, with international perspective, knowledge of civil engineering, as well as systematic and open knowledge structure composed of basic and professional knowledge of management, economics and law related to domestic and international engineering management, which can accept basic training of engineers, meanwhile have strong professional quality and ability, practical ability, innovation ability, and possess healthy personality and good social adaptability, able to work on project decision-making and domestic and foreign civil engineering And other engineering areas of engineering decision-making, whole process of project management and related professional management in the field of domestic and foreign engineering projects and other projects.

Ⅱ. Requirements of Professional Training
Students mainly study the basic theory and knowledge of civil engineering and other necessary engineering technology, management, economics and law, and would thoroughly and systematically get trained in scientific thought, system thinking, management thought, cultural thinking and engineers, finally having knowledge management ability, knowledge application ability and innovation ability; possessing comprehensive professional ability to discover, analyze, research and deal with practical problems of project management; possessing fundamental ability for engineering planning, design management, investment control, schedule control, quality control, safety management, contract management, information management, organization and coordination. Graduates should obtain following knowledge, ability and quality: 1. Master basic knowledge of civil engineering technology, familiar with other necessary knowledge of engineering and technology; 2. Master management theory and management methods related to domestic and international engineering management, relevant economic theories and methods and relevant laws and regulations; 3. Master fundamental professional knowledge, professional knowledge, expertise and methods of domestic and international engineering management field; 4. Possess basic ability to comprehensively use theories, knowledge, techniques and methods of above aspects to work on domestic and international engineering technology management, professional management, integrated management and whole process management, have basic ability to perform general civil engineering design; 5. Possess basic ability to read, write and translate professional foreign language literature of engineering management; 6. Have basic ability to deal with engineering management problems and related issues with computer; 7. Possess primary scientific ability, have strong expressive ability and interpersonal communication skills, have healthy personality traits and good social adaptability; 8. Understand the latest developments and trend of theory and practice in the field of domestic and international engineering management; 9. Have theoretical knowledge required by engineering management (construction) professional national qualification of relevant industries and fields.

Ⅲ. Education System and Degrees Conferred
Education System 4 years, Bachelor of Management

Ⅳ. Major Disciplines
Management science and engineering, civil engineering

Ⅴ. Major Courses
1. Discipline Fundamental Courses: advanced mathematics (Ⅰ), advanced mathematics (Ⅱ), linear algebra, probability statistics, engineering mechanics, operations research, engineering drawing and mapping, housing architecture, engineering structure, construction technology and organization, professional introduction. 2. Professional Core Courses: engineering economics, engineering contract management, project valuation, project management, project cost planning and control. 3. Professional Elective Courses: introduction to engineering management, building materials, construction CAD, construction law and regulation, management, installation engineering technology, economic law, accounting, engineering surveying, engineering supervision, western economics, project risk management, groundwork and foundation, engineering management software and application, project cost application software, BIM software and its application, engineering information management, engineering financial management, organizational behavior, HRM, mine engineering technology, municipal public engineering technology, highway engineering technology, water conservancy and hydropower engineering, real estate economics, real estate marketing, real estate development, real estate theory and practice, FIDIC contract conditions (bilingual), construction enterprise management, project schedule planning and control, building quality and safety management, construction equipment, finance and insurance , statistics application, international practice of engineering management.
